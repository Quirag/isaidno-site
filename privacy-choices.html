Privacy choices

Three toggles, all yours. None of them require contacting us.

1. Anonymous analytics

By default, anonymous usage analytics are OFF. If you want to help improve the app by sharing anonymous interaction signals (e.g. "logged a no," "opened the library"), turn it ON in:

iPhone → I Said No → Settings → Privacy → Anonymous analytics

What's sent: a random UUID generated on your device + event names. What's never sent: your name, email, journal content, notes, photos, voice transcripts, or calendar contents. The analytics processor is PostHog, hosted in the EU.

2. Reset / delete your data

Tap Settings → Reset all data inside the app. This wipes every entry, body cue, custom category, photo, and transcript stored on the device. Once tapped, the deletion is permanent — there is no backend copy.

3. Export your data

Tap Settings → Export data (JSON) for a full machine-readable export, or Settings → Therapist export (PDF) for a summary. Both are generated on-device and saved to your iOS Files app.

iOS-level controls

You can revoke any of these at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security:

  • Calendars → I Said No (if calendar prep was enabled)
  • Microphone → I Said No (if voice memos were enabled)
  • Camera → I Said No (if photo journaling was enabled)
  • Photos → I Said No (if you attached a photo)
  • Speech Recognition → I Said No
  • Notifications → I Said No

The app fails gracefully on any feature whose permission you've revoked — nothing crashes, nothing nags.

GDPR / CCPA

The app has no backend, so your data has always been under your direct control. The above three toggles are the complete set of "data rights" available — exercise them in-app, no email needed. Full details in the Privacy Policy.
