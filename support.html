Frequently asked

How do I cancel my subscription?

All subscriptions are managed by Apple. Open iPhone Settings → tap your name at the top → Subscriptions → I Said No → Cancel Subscription. Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period.

How do I get a refund?

Refunds are handled by Apple, not by us. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com and select the I Said No purchase. Refund decisions are Apple's.

I lost my data. Can you recover it?

No. The app has no backend. Your journal lives only on your device. If you restored your iPhone from an Apple-encrypted iCloud or local backup, the app data should come back automatically. If you reset all data inside the app, that delete is permanent.

How do I export my journal?

In the app: Settings → Export data (JSON) for everything, or Settings → Therapist export (PDF) for a summary you can share. Both are generated on-device.

I'm switching to a new iPhone. Will my journal follow?

If you set up the new iPhone from an encrypted iCloud or local backup of the old one, yes — your data restores along with the app. If you set up "as new" or "Quick Start" without a backup, no — there is no cloud copy on our side.

Does I Said No work on iPad / Mac?

The first version ships iPhone-only. Apple Watch is a companion. iPad and Mac may come later.

Does the app share my data?

No. Read the Privacy Policy for the long version. Short version: nothing leaves your device by default. Optional anonymous usage analytics can be toggled off in Settings → Privacy.

Where do the references in the Library come from?

Each Library essay carries a footer citing the source — Pete Walker's Complex PTSD: From Surviving to Thriving, Marsha Linehan's DBT manuals, IFS literature (Schwartz), Bessel van der Kolk's The Body Keeps the Score, and a few others. The reflections in the app are journaling prompts inspired by that work — not therapy, not medical advice, not a diagnostic tool.

If you're in crisis, please reach a hotline. 988 (US), 116 123 (UK Samaritans), or findahelpline.com worldwide. This app is not equipped to handle a crisis, but those people are.
